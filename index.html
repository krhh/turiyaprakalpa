What Are We Solving: India’s Innovation Crisis

We have failed to create a problem-solving mindset in India’s youth. Our 43.3 million students (AISHE 2021-22) are trapped in a cycle of rote learning and job-chasing, disconnected from regional pain points like Bihar’s floods or Jharkhand’s healthcare gaps. Only 10% of colleges offer AI programs (NASSCOM 2023), and India lags in deeptech innovation, with just 3,000 deeptech startups compared to 30,000 in the U.S. (FICCI-Deloitte 2025). This is not a pipeline problem—it’s a mindset crisis.

Why This Crisis Exists:

  • Late Global Exposure: Indian youth are tail-enders, adopting technologies like AI years after the West, with minimal access to breakthroughs (e.g., only 16% of global AI talent, Bain & Capital 2022).
  • Zero Regional Exposure: Curricula ignore local challenges & pain-points, leaving students unaware of solvable problems.
  • No Encouragement to Innovate: Risk-averse education systems prioritize jobs over entrepreneurship, stifling original ideas.

Impact of the Crisis:

“We are workers, not innovators.”

India’s entrepreneurial ventures often mimic Western models, not deeptech solutions. Most educated youth waste years chasing jobs, with only 4% of graduates starting businesses (NASSCOM 2023). Less than 1% tackle societal challenges, leaving regional pain points unresolved and India’s $7.8 billion AI market (IBEF 2023) underutilized.

Why Start with AI?

AI Revolution – Urgent Mass Enablement

The AI revolution is here, and India risks falling behind. The U.S. trains 500,000 AI professionals annually, China’s AI Education Plan reaches 100,000+ schools (WEF 2025), while India’s 1 million AI job demand by 2026 (Wheebox 2023) faces a 90% skilling gap (NASSCOM 2023). AI is becoming fundamental, reshaping 70% of jobs by 2030 (McKinsey 2025). Without urgent mass enablement, India’s youth will be relegated to low-skill roles in a $45.72 billion AI market (MarketResearchFuture 2025). Turiya Prakalpa is the answer, starting now.
